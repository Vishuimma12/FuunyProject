A Letter for You

Muskan,

I wanted to make you something today for no reason at all — no anniversary, no special date, nothing to celebrate except the very ordinary, wonderful fact that you exist and that I get to love you. Sometimes the quietest days deserve the softest reminders.

I don't think I say this enough, so let me say it plainly: you make my life feel lighter. Somewhere between the small things — the way you laugh at your own jokes before you've finished telling them, the voice notes that turn a boring afternoon into something I look forward to, the way you show up for the people you love without ever making it about yourself — I stopped seeing you as someone I'm lucky to have, and started seeing you as home.

I love you on the easy days and I love you on the hard ones. I love the version of you that has it all figured out, and the version that's still figuring it out — because both of them are yours, and I want all of it.

This little page is small, and it definitely doesn't come close to how much you mean to me, but I wanted you to have something that's just yours to open whenever you need reminding: you are loved, completely and on purpose, every single day — not just today.

— Vishal
